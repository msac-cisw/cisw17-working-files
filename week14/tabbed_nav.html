Winter

Winter, the “Season of Silence,” descends upon the world like a silver frost. It is said that the Snow Queen, with her cloak of stars, blankets the land in quiet splendor. Icy winds, thought to be her whispered songs, sweep through the barren trees. The nights grow long, and the world slumbers under a quilt of snow. Winter is often seen as a time of stillness and introspection in fairy tales, where hearths glow warmly against the cold and stories are shared to keep the darkness at bay. The season symbolizes both an ending and a promise of new beginnings to come.

Spring

Spring, often called the “Season of Blossoms,” is a time when the world awakens from its wintry slumber. In this season, the Earth is said to don a gown of emerald green, with flowers blooming as if summoned by an ancient spell. Gentle rains, known as the “Tears of the Sky,” nourish the land, while birds sing melodies gifted by the Wind Spirit. According to legend, the Queen of Spring touches the trees, bringing forth leaves and blossoms, symbolizing renewal and hope. Spring is often associated with themes of rebirth, youth, and the triumph of life over the cold grasp of winter.

Summer

Summer, the “Season of the Sun,” is a time of warmth and abundance, when the days stretch long under golden skies. In myth, the Sun King rides high across the heavens, pouring out his light to nurture the land. Fields of wheat sway like oceans of gold, and forests hum with the buzz of enchanted creatures. Lakes and rivers sparkle, enchanted by the laughter of water sprites. Summer is traditionally a time of celebration and adventure, where tales often speak of heroes embarking on quests beneath the ever-watchful eye of the bright sun.

Fall / Autumn

Autumn, or the “Season of Harvest,” is a time when the world is cloaked in shades of amber and crimson. It is whispered that the Forest Spirits paint each leaf with care before they fall. This season marks the balance between light and darkness, as the days grow shorter and the air turns crisp. According to legend, Autumn is the domain of the Harvest Witch, who blesses the fields and forests with bountiful crops. Stories often depict Autumn as a time of reflection and preparation, where wisdom is gathered like the last fruits of the vine before winter’s arrival.
